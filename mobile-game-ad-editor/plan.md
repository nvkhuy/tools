# Mobile Game CPI Video Plan

Create five approximately 30-second puzzle-game ads. Each video should focus on one clear creative intention while varying the opening, pacing, layout, pressure, or ending.

## Video 1 — Combo

**Intention:** Build satisfaction through an escalating combo.

**Scenario:**

1. Show two simple moves to explain the gameplay.
2. Follow with several faster successful moves that build a combo.
3. Finish on the win screen.

**Variation to test:** Add a short intro before the gameplay.

## Video 2 — Immense Gameplay

**Intention:** Emphasize the scale of the puzzle and large chain reactions.

**Scenario:**

1. Open by revealing a large, full board.
2. Show multiple moves to establish the gameplay loop.
3. Trigger mass reactions across the board.

**Variation to test:** Change the game-board layout.

## Video 3 — Edging Fail

**Intention:** Build suspense, then end in failure.

**Scenario:**

1. Show two moves to explain the gameplay.
2. Pause briefly to suggest hesitation before the next decision.
3. Make a good move.
4. Pause again before a more difficult decision.
5. Make a bad move.
6. End on the fail screen.

**Variation to test:** Add a banner that increases tension or clarifies the challenge.

## Video 4 — Edging Win

**Intention:** Build suspense in the same way as Video 3, but deliver a successful ending.

**Scenario:**

1. Show two moves to explain the gameplay.
2. Pause briefly to suggest hesitation.
3. Make a good move.
4. Pause again before the final decision.
5. Make another good move.
6. End on the win screen.

**Variation to test:** Increase gameplay speed.

## Video 5 — Find Something

**Intention:** Turn the puzzle into a visual search challenge.

**Scenario:** Define a specific object, match, or solution for the viewer to find within the board. Keep the target visible and make the search understandable without sound.

**Variation to test:** Add time pressure, such as a visible countdown or urgency cue.

## Shared Production Direction

- Explain the core mechanic through visible moves before escalating it.
- Keep each video centered on one intention rather than mixing several concepts.
- Use hesitation deliberately in the fail/win suspense variants.
- Make combo growth, mass reactions, good moves, and bad moves visually distinct.
- Ensure win and fail endings are immediately recognizable.
- Treat intros, banners, layouts, speed, and time pressure as testable variations rather than mandatory elements in every video.
